Welcome to my personal webpage! I've recently earned my Master's in Computational Physics and I'm looking forward to the next step in my career. I'm interested in software engineering and scientific computing jobs, particularly in roles which would leverage my strong background in data analysis and numerical computing.

The foundation of my career is my four years in the US Air Force. As a Seismic Data Analyst at the Air Force Technical Applications Center (AFTAC), I interpreted data from geophysical sensors across the globe in support of the Air Force's nuclear treaty monitoring mission. My role also allowed me to explore data engineering practices and streamlining of workflows with bash and Python scripts.

After my military service, I studied physics at California State University Long Beach - first as an undergrad and then as a Master's candidate. My research in the group of Dr. Andreas Bill concerns a particular arrangement of graphene known as twisted bilayer graphene (TBLG). The recent discovery of superconductivity in TBLG at certain "magic" twist angles defies easy explanation. My thesis, titled The Plasmon Spectrum of Twisted Bilayer Graphene, explores one possible explanation of this phenomenon in the form of acoustic plasmons. I developed high-performance Python code to compute the complex band structure of TBLG and characterize the behavior of plasmons in the system.
